  • EQUITY INITIATIVES
    We are grounded in social justice and embrace the assets of our diverse communities.
    UW Tacoma prides itself on the diversity of our students, and the enriching campus life and learning such diversity brings. Yet our staff and faculty ranks do not reflect a comparable composition. Systematically assessing the experiences of students, faculty and staff will allow us to take evidence-based actions to support their retention and success. Embracing the changing demographics of higher education will help us move closer to fulfilling the promise of our vision and values. As we do so, we will become an engaged, civil and just university.
    Contact a Co-Champion: James McShay or Linda Ishem
  • IMPROVING FACULTY DIVERSITY THROUGH RECRUIT/RETAIN PROCESS
    We will examine our current recruitment and retention processes to determine strengths and opportunities during the faculty recruitment cycle. Specifically, we will redesign our search ads, committee review processes and salary adjustment processes to be more inclusive with the goal of having our faculty body mirror our student body.
  • INTERNAL CEI PROGRAM REVIEW
    The Center for Equity & Inclusion will engage in a multiphase internal review process to develop a comprehensive five-year plan to guide its practice and focus its energy on objectives and goals.
    CEI's internal review process will be organized around four distinct phases of activity that build upon one another. Phase 1 will focus on visioning and mission clarification; inventory of programs and services; and a review of CEI's current assessment plan. Phase 2 will focus on strategic goal development around CEI's functional areas. Phases 3 & 4 will involve looking at opportunities for emerging a research agenda as well as look at how it might work to evolve an enhanced brand identity based upon its soon to be created strategic goals.

  • ASSET MAPPING PROJECT (DIVERSITY, EQUITY, AND INCLUSION)
    20 UW Tacoma senior leaders including the Chancellor’s Cabinet and Deans & Directors will participate in an eight-week cohort-based Institute hosted by USC Race & Equity Center and funded in part by the Lumina Foundation. It focuses on racial equity and is designed to allow for sustained high-level thinking and engagement around ways they can work together to strategically advance UW Tacoma’s equity and inclusion agenda.
    Areas of study for the participants will align with UW Tacoma priorities and initiatives and cover topics such as:
    • Racial Equity
    • Race-Conscious Leadership in Higher Education
    • Assessment, Evaluation, and Racial Equity
    • College Teaching & Learning
    • Racial Equity and Human Resource Management
    • Administration and Management of Racial Equity
    • Contemporary Issues in Campus and Institutional Equity
  • ACT ON EQUITY DATA GOALS
    The July 2018, The Equity Data Working Group submitted a report that examined the following: how can UW Tacoma build capacity and intentionality behind the question ‘If we were a more equitable campus, what would it look like? What would we measure? How would we know we were more equitable?’
    The working group examined assessment and accountability practices for equity in higher education and surveyed available UW Tacoma data and current reporting practices and proposed the creation of a Charting Our Course Dashboard which could be used as a tool to help track progress and increase transparency around the work that still needs to be done.
  • ENHANCE SERVICES FOR INTERNATIONAL STUDENTS
    As UW Tacoma works to strategically increase their number of international students, OGA will be responsible for maintaining Visa compliance, international student orientation, programming for cultural immersion, post-graduation OPT support, and many other services.
  • SEED
    SEED is a faculty development program (open to all ranks, including full-time lecturers) to help faculty members build and deliver university courses that utilize practices for an inclusive classroom. Through this work we strive to ensure that we fulfill the mission and values of UWT, particularly excellence, diversity, and community.
    SEED begins with a 4-day summer Teaching Institute. Faculty members will come together to rethink and redesign courses and program offerings. The institute addresses concerns related to student success, equity, intersectionality, anti-racist education, accessibility, collaboration, discrimination, and inclusive pedagogy.
  • MOVE CENTER FOR EQUITY & INCLUSION
    Campus Planning, McGranahan Architects, and staff from the Center from the Center for Equity & Inclusion will work together over the 2018-19 academic year to design a space that best meets the needs of all students through fostering critical interactions between faculty, staff and the broader Tacoma community. This design process follows many months of consultations with a number of student groups and campus partners about CEI’s use and possibilities for extending its reach and impact in promoting an affirming and inclusive UWT community.
    The plan is to move CEI to a new larger community facility that is designed to serve a major hub of campus engagement offering programs, activities and resources designed to meet the needs of all UWT students through fostering on-going purposeful interaction with faculty, staff and members of the Tacoma community and broader region.
  • FIRST GENERATION
    SAES will partner with Academic Affairs and student leaders to enhance and increase supports for first generation students to meet their specific needs and enhance their experience, to increase retention and to promote a graduate-degree seeking culture.
