  • STUDENT INITIATIVES
    The UW Tacoma experience empowers students to achieve their dreams.
    Educating learners is why we exist. Our alumni’s accomplishments in their lives, their careers and their service to our communities are the ultimate measures of our university’s success. Achieving this takes the whole university, the foundation of which is excellent teaching. The range and depth of our support for students and the quality of experiences available to them outside the classroom completes this commitment.

  • DRESSEL SCHOLARSHIP
    Because so many UW Tacoma students are first-generation students who possibly don’t know the pathways to engagement in their community—or don’t have access to those networks—this scholarship and mentorship program is designed to open doors. The financial assistance for Dressel Scholars is designed to free up students’ time so that they can engage in ways that will plant their roots here and weave them into the fabric of this community. Our goal is to create a world-class scholars program that is good for students, good for the community, and honors the legacy of leadership that Melanie Dressel embodied.
  • DEVELOP E-LEARNING PLAN 2.0
    Inform and inspire: eLearning growth at UW Tacoma. Create an initiative at UWT that incentivizes new pedagogy in teaching and learning. This would include:
    1. priority classroom use for courses at >50% hybrid
    2. web site that captures the success of early adopters (iTech Fellows)
    3. working with deans/directors to explore solutions to sticky problems within their discipline (low enrollment, low learner success, high wait list courses, etc).
  • SUPPORT REVISION OF FIRST YEAR CORE
    Redesign of the First Year Core curriculum to develop pathways that guide students to majors and support holistic learning outcomes across all courses. Engage faculty campus wide in creating courses and curriculum that support student success. Assure ownership of the courses by faculty and academic units to improve continuity and quality of learning experience.
  • LAUNCHING MAKER SPACE
    The Maker Space will be an intentionally designed, impactful, informal learning space that enables design thinking, creativity, hands-on learning, and making at its core. Though we envision a future with a fully featured Maker Space, we anticipate the UW Tacoma Maker Space will start out as a flexible meeting space and will evolve in an iterative way to meet the developing needs of this campus.
  • LEVERAGE DATA ANALYTICS - STUDENT SCHEDULING & CURRICULUM
    Determine analytic tool capacity - Civitas, UW tools in pipeline - to deliver 1) student-facing and 2) institutional predictors and data discovery tools related to use in scheduling and curriculum. Focus will be on possibilities for real-time planning, discovering impediments, promoting practices in student success.

These will be reviewed and prioritized by the Academic Policy and Curriculum Committee and discussed by the Council of Deans and Directors to establish which programs should move forward in the full curriculum review process.

  • UW TACOMA HUSKY EXPERIENCE
    SAES will partner with Academic Affairs and student leaders to define the UW Tacoma Husky Experience - a roadmap for our diverse students to plan a meaningful and highly integrated student experience that helps them to develop intellectually, socially, professionally and civically.
  • OFFER INCLUSIVE PEDAGOGY TRAINING FOR FACULTY
    This will be a collaborative effort across campus to identify those at UW Tacoma who have experience and expertise in inclusive pedagogy while identifying experts in our community, the UW system and throughout higher education. We will develop training for all faculty that will support them in the creation of learning environments that are inclusive an equitable.

    The Office of Global Affairs will continue its efforts to expand opportunities for and access to study abroad programs. This includes a focus on maintaining equitable access for all students, supporting faculty in the development and delivery of innovative and immersive programs, and collaboration with units across campus such as the Center for Equity and Inclusion.
  • CREATE INNOVATION CAPACITY IN ALIGNMENT WITH GLOBAL ENGAGEMENT
    After a Summer 2018 visit to the Stanford Design School by key external partners and campus leadership, the Institute for Global Engagement was asked to lead an initiative on Innovation and Design at UW Tacoma. A design-thinking framework will guide curriculum and partnerships to further promote UW Tacoma's urban serving mission.
  • EVALUATE AND ENHANCE STRATEGIC STUDENT COMMUNICATIONS
    There are a number of units across campus that reach out to students with reminders and nudges to improve their success. There is some, but not enough coordination among these groups to avoid redundancy, inconsistency, and most importantly, gaps. This cross-divisional group plans to meet to work together, building on existing and recent programs such as Persistence Plus, EAB Guide, and others, to coordinate and strategically and intentionally reach out to students with an eye towards their retention and success.
  • POST-GRADUATE PATHWAYS AND PERSPECTIVES ON THE UW EXPERIENCE
    SAES will partner with Academic Affairs to understand UW Tacoma alumi’s post-graduate pathways and perspectives on the UW experience and to use our understanding to develop our admissions, academic advising and career development efforts and to enhance the UW Tacoma Husky Experience.
  • SCHOLARSHIP SUPPORT
    SAES will assess the use and impact of unrestricted merit and need-based scholarship support to increase the amount allocated to students and to refine the fundraising case for financial support.
